# Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhash Chandra Bose, popularly known as **Netaji**, was one of India's most influential freedom fighters and a prominent leader in the struggle for independence from British rule. He was born on **January 23, 1897**, in Cuttack, Odisha.

Bose was known for his strong patriotism, determination, and leadership qualities. He believed that India's freedom could be achieved through active resistance against British rule. During World War II, he founded and led the **Indian National Army (INA)**, also known as the Azad Hind Fauj, with the aim of liberating India from colonial rule.

His famous slogan, **"Give me blood, and I will give you freedom,"** inspired thousands of Indians to join the independence movement. He also established the **Provisional Government of Free India (Azad Hind Government)** to strengthen the fight for independence.

Subhash Chandra Bose's courage, sacrifice, and dedication continue to inspire generations of Indians. He remains a symbol of patriotism, leadership, and unwavering commitment to the nation's freedom.

## Key Contributions
- Leader of the Indian independence movement.
- Founder and Supreme Commander of the Indian National Army (INA).
- Established the Azad Hind Government.
- Inspired millions through his speeches and vision for a free India.
- Played a significant role in strengthening the struggle against British rule.

## Legacy
Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose is remembered as one of India's greatest national heroes. His vision, bravery, and sacrifice continue to inspire people across the world.
